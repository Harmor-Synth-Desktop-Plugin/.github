## 🚀 Introduction
Harmor for Windows is an additive synthesizer plugin from Image-Line, the studio behind FL Studio. Its engine describes sound as thousands of partials, which means it can not only synthesize tones from scratch but also resynthesize existing audio and translate images into sound. This image-based approach opens up sound design that ranges from crisp classic-style patches to sprawling, otherworldly textures, all within a single instrument that Windows producers can drop into their favorite host.

## ✨ Highlights

### Sound Creation
- **Additive synthesis engine** — Sculpt sound one partial at a time for exact control over harmonics and timbre.
- **Resynthesis of audio and images** — Import a sample or picture and reconstruct it as editable additive data.
- **Flexible sound design** — Two independent parts, deep modulation, and effects support intricate, evolving patches.

### Shaping and Movement
- **Advanced filters** — Phase-accurate filtering that works on the partials themselves for smooth, expressive sweeps.
- **Unison** — Build wide, thick voices with detailed control over spread, detune, and phase.

## 🎯 Getting Up and Running
1. Start FL Studio, Ableton Live, or another compatible host on your Windows PC.
2. Insert Harmor as a VST instrument on a track or channel.
3. Audition the factory presets to learn the engine's range.
4. Edit partials, filters, and unison to personalize a sound.
5. Drag in audio or an image and use resynthesis to reshape it into something new.

## ❓ Common Questions
**Is Harmor free?**
Harmor is a paid instrument by Image-Line. It comes bundled with certain FL Studio editions and is also offered as a paid add-on, and you can try it via the FL Studio trial. No free standalone full copy exists, so any "harmor vst free download" you find should be read with that in mind.

**Which versions of Windows are supported?**
Harmor supports current 64-bit versions of Windows, including Windows 10 and Windows 11, inside FL Studio or another VST-capable host such as Ableton Live.

**Is my data secure?**
Harmor runs locally as a plugin on your Windows computer, so your presets, projects, and audio remain on your own system.
